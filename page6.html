Encrypt and decrypt a message

ROT13 is a simple letter substitution cipher that replaces a letter with the 13th letter after it in the Latin alphabet.

ROT13 Table:

To encrypt the message "helsinki" using ROT13, we replace each letter with the letter 13 positions ahead in the alphabet:

  • "h" becomes "u"
  • "e" becomes "r"
  • "l" becomes "y"
  • "s" becomes "e"
  • "i" becomes "v"
  • "n" becomes "a"
  • "k" becomes "x"
  • "i" becomes "v"

So, the ROT13 encryption of "helsinki" is "uryevaxv."

To decrypt "uryevaxv" back to "helsinki," we apply ROT13 again:

  • "u" becomes "h"
  • "r" becomes "e"
  • "y" becomes "l"
  • "e" becomes "s"
  • "v" becomes "i"
  • "a" becomes "n"
  • "x" becomes "k"
  • "v" becomes "i"

The result is the original word "helsinki."

So, "uryevaxv" decrypts to "helsinki" when using ROT13.
